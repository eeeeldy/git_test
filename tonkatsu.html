Tonkatsu

Chicken katsu is Japanese-style fried chicken. This is my family recipe and can also be used to make tonkatsu by using pork cutlets instead of chicken. Serve with white rice and tonkatsu sauce.
Make restaurant-worthy chicken katsu at home with this top-rated recipe. You won't believe how quickly it comes together with simple ingredients!

Here's What you need:

  • skinless, boneless chicken thighs (about 6 ounces each)
  • Kosher salt
  • cup all-purpose flour
  • large eggs, lightly beaten with 3 tablespoons of water
  • cups panko breadcrumbs
  • cups vegetable oil, divided
  • Tonkatsu sauce, for serving (see Note)
  • Dijon mustard, for serving
  • Steamed sushi rice, for serving)

Procedures:

  1. Gather the ingredients.
  2. Lightly pound the chicken thighs 1/2 inch-thick and season with salt.
  3. Put the flour, eggs, and panko in three separate pie plates and season each one lightly with salt.
  4. Dredge 1 piece of chicken in the flour, tapping off the excess.
  5. Dip in the egg, allowing the excess to drip off, then coat the chicken with the panko, pressing to help it adhere.
  6. Transfer the chicken to a wax paperâ€“lined baking sheet. Repeat with the remaining chicken.
  7. Divide the oil between two large skillets and heat until shimmering. Add the chicken and fry over moderately high heat, turning once, until golden and crispy, 3 minutes per side. Drain on paper towels.
  8. Serve the chicken with tonkatsu sauce, mustard, and steamed rice
